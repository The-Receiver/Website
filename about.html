Who are we?

We, Myanmar Children Health Project (MCHP), are a student-led not-for-profit
organization committed to maintaining the overall health and welfare of children
living in rural areas of Myanmar through vaccinations provided by certified doctors.
As of now we are currently focused on Hepatitis B.

What do we do?

Every month, we host three vaccination events at a designated area. In the first event,
certified doctors from Myanmar Pharmaceutical Industries for Hepatitis B come to test the
children for Hepatitis B and provide vaccinations against Hepatitis B. In the second event,
which is a month later, the children get vaccinated again. The third event is the same but
occurs six months after the first event. During the events, MCHP members comfort crying
children and socialize with them. In addition to that, MCHP members educate the community
about essential health information such as sanitation and nutrition. The task of vaccinating

Why Hepatitis B?

Hepatitis B is an infection that harms the liver which is spread through contact with body
fluids. It can be easily transmitted through infected blood products such as needles, razors,
and unscreened blood transfusions. Although there is no cure for acute Hepatitis B, it can
be prevented through vaccinations. We are currently focused on Hepatitis B due to the lack of
government attention in its prevention, as the prevention of most other fatal diseases in
Myanmar are sponsored by the Burmese government.

Our goal

To vaccinate 5,000 children for the prevention of Hepatitis B by 2022
